Federico Tartarini

Postdoctoral Scholar at Berkeley Education Alliance for Research in Singapore (BEARS) federicotartarini@gmail.com

The focus of my work, as a Postdoctoral Scholar, has mostly been on thermal comfort, Indoor Environmental Quality (IEQ), Heating Ventilation and Air Conditioning (HVAC) systems and development of non-intrusive methodologies to monitor IEQ parameters.

I received my PhD in 2017 from UOW during which I focused on determining the impact of IEQ on perceptions of occupants (staff and residents) and agitated behaviors of residents at aged care facilities. I am actively collaborating with the International Energy Agency EBC Annex 69 "Strategy and Practice of Adaptive Thermal Comfort in Low Energy Buildings".

I am the author of the Python package pythermalcomfort and of the BLE Android application named BEARS. I have also significantly contributed to the following projects: CBE thermal comfort tool and Cozie.app
